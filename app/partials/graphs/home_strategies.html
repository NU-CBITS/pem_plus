Strategies and Options for Home


The Things You've Tried


Basic care routines

  1. ASL: supporting spoken language with signs helps his comprehension a great deal.
  2. Routine: Pete does best when he knows what to expect, e.g., we almost always eat at the same times, in the same place, together.

Household chores

  1. Pete loves to cook—but he needs a lot of supervision and support. But then again, he’s four. So working very closely with him helps.
  2. If you want Pete to be helpful, you need to keep reminding him of what we’re doing, usually by singing a song about cleaning [or what have you].

Interactive and organized play￼

  1. Right now, using visual cues and PECS.

Socializing with friends and family

  1. I try to make sure that I have ways to let Pete be distracted—a weighted vest, stim toys.
  2. Strategic withdrawal: trying to let Pete get away from people with me when he gets overwhelmed.
